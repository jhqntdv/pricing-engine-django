03. EXOTICS DOCUMENTATION (PATH-DEPENDENT & EARLY EXERCISE)

1. PRODUCT OVERVIEW

DEFINITION: Exotic Options are derivatives with features that make them more complex than standard vanilla options. This engine primarily focuses on Path-Dependent Options and Early Exercise Options (American/Bermudan).

KEY CATEGORIES:

  • BARRIER OPTIONS (Up/Down & In/Out): Knock-in or Knock-out depending on whether a barrier price level is breached during the life of the option. Typically cheaper than vanilla options due to conditional payoffs.
  • ASIAN OPTIONS: Payoff depends on the average price of the underlying over a specified period. The averaging process reduces volatility impact, making them cheaper than standard options. Popular in commodities and FX hedging.
  • LOOKBACK OPTIONS: Payoff depends on the maximum or minimum price reached by the underlying during the life of the option. Extremely expensive as they provide the "hindsight" optimal price.
  • EARLY EXERCISE (AMERICAN / BERMUDAN): Options that allow the holder to exercise before maturity. American options allow continuous exercise, while Bermudan options restrict exercise to predefined discrete dates.

2. PRICING CALCULATOR INPUTS & SETTINGS

  • MODEL COMPARISON (BS vs HESTON): Pricing path-dependent exotics heavily benefits from testing across both Black-Scholes and Heston models. The Heston model's stochastic volatility significantly alters the probability of extreme path movements (fat tails), which directly and heavily impacts Barrier breach probabilities and Lookback maximums.
  • VOLATILITY SURFACE (SSVI MANDATORY): Path generation requires extracting implied volatilities continuously across time and strikes. SSVI (Surface Stochastic Volatility Inspired) is strictly mandated here to prevent local volatility collapse when evaluating simulated paths near barriers.
  • PATH & STEP DENSITY: Path dependency requires a high density of time steps (e.g., 250+ steps per year, representing daily observations) to accurately capture barrier breaches or moving average calculations. High nb_paths (50,000+) is required to converge the simulation variance.

3. QUANTITATIVE ENGINE: MONTE CARLO & LSM

Closed-form analytical solutions are generally unavailable or highly restricted for these complex payoffs. The system relies entirely on Monte Carlo (MC) simulation and Least Squares regression:

  • PATH GENERATION: Simulates the underlying asset using Euler Discretization under the risk-neutral measure.
  • PATH-DEPENDENT PAYOFF EVALUATION:
        - Barrier Options: The path loop sequentially checks if S(t) crosses the defined Barrier.
        - Asian Options: The engine accumulates Sum(S(t)) over the observation period and calculates the Average(S) at maturity.
        - Lookback Options: The engine dynamically tracks Max(S(t)) or Min(S(t)) throughout the entire simulated path.
  • LONGSTAFF-SCHWARTZ METHOD (LSM) FOR EARLY EXERCISE:
    To price American and Bermudan options via Monte Carlo, the engine employs the Least Squares Monte Carlo (LSM) algorithm.
        - Working backwards from maturity to present, the engine performs a cross-sectional polynomial regression at each exercise date.
        - It regresses the discounted future payoffs (from holding the option) against the current simulated asset prices to estimate the Continuation Value.
        - If the Intrinsic Value of exercising immediately is strictly greater than the estimated Continuation Value, the option is marked as exercised on that specific path.

METRIC / RISK EXOTIC / EARLY EXERCISE CHARACTERISTIC
NPV (FAIR VALUE) Highly sensitive to the chosen volatility model. Heston NPVs often diverge significantly from Black-Scholes NPVs for Lookbacks and out-of-the-money Barriers due to the skew impact on tail probabilities.
DELTA & GAMMA DISCONTINUITIES For Barrier options (especially Knock-Outs), Delta and Gamma behave erratically near the barrier. Approaching the barrier, the option value can drop to zero instantly, creating extreme Gamma exposure (Gamma Pin Risk) that is incredibly difficult to delta-hedge dynamically.
EARLY EXERCISE PREMIUM The difference between the American/Bermudan NPV and the equivalent European NPV represents the early exercise premium. This premium is highly sensitive to the dividend yield and interest rates.
SYSTEM WARNING: Running Greeks (Bump-and-Reval) on exotics under the Heston model requires recalculating paths with double the stochastic variables (Price and Variance paths). For LSM, the backward regression must also be rerun for each bump. This operation is computationally heavy and should be queued to background workers.
