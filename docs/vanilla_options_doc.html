02. VANILLA OPTIONS DOCUMENTATION

1. PRODUCT OVERVIEW

DEFINITION: European Vanilla Options (Calls and Puts) give the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell the underlying asset at a specified strike price on the maturity date.

USE CASES: Primary building blocks for structured products. Used by trading desks for directional speculation, volatility trading, and dynamic hedging.

2. VALUATION MODELS

MODEL DESCRIPTION PRODUCT CONTROL PERSPECTIVE
BLACK-SCHOLES (BS) Standard diffusion model assuming constant volatility and log-normal asset price distribution. Industry standard for basic quotation and implied volatility surface construction. Fails to capture volatility smile/skew.
HESTON MODEL Stochastic volatility model where variance follows a Cox-Ingersoll-Ross (CIR) mean-reverting process. Critical for accurate risk representation of long-dated options. Captures volatility smile/skew and forward volatility dynamics.

3. REQUIRED INPUTS (MARKET & TRADE DATA)

  • TRADE PARAMETERS: Strike Price (K), Maturity / Time to Expiry (T).
  • SPOT PRICE (S): Current market price of the underlying asset.
  • YIELD CURVE (r): Discounting rates (e.g., SOFR, US Treasury, OAT) fitted using Cubic or Svensson interpolation methods.
  • VOLATILITY SURFACE (σ): Market implied volatilities interpolated using SVI (Stochastic Volatility Inspired) or SSVI models to prevent arbitrage.
  • DAY COUNT CONVENTION: Time discretization logic (e.g., ACT/360, ACT/365) to accurately compute accrual factors.

4. OUTPUTS & RISK MANAGEMENT (GREEKS)

Product control and risk management rely on the NPV and Greeks to monitor limits, explain P&L, and hedge exposures.

[ PLACEHOLDER: GREEKS SENSITIVITY CHART / PAYOFF DIAGRAM ]
METRIC DEFINITION RISK MANAGEMENT FOCUS
NPV (FAIR VALUE) Theoretical present value of the option. Mark-to-Market (MTM) book valuation. Daily P&L calculation.
DELTA (Δ) Sensitivity to underlying spot price changes. Directional risk. Used to calculate the delta-hedge ratio (shares to buy/sell).
GAMMA (Γ) Rate of change of Delta. Convexity. Re-hedging cost and gap risk overnight. High gamma indicates volatile P&L.
VEGA (ν) Sensitivity to 1% change in implied volatility. Exposure to market panic/euphoria. Key limit for option market makers.
THETA (Θ) Time decay. Change in value per day passing. Cost of carrying the option position. Typically bleeds value for long options.
RHO (ρ) Sensitivity to 1% change in interest rates. Funding cost risk. Higher impact on long-dated options.
