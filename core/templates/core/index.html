01. SYSTEM OVERVIEW & MODEL RISK

STATUS: [ ONLINE ]
COMPLIANCE: ALIGNED WITH ENTERPRISE VALUATION CONTROL POLICIES

This platform serves as the centralized Quantitative Pricing Engine for equity derivatives and structured products. Designed under the Model Risk Management (MRM) framework, the engine ensures pricing transparency, reproducibility, and consistency across trading and control functions. By integrating market-calibrated volatility surfaces (SVI/SSVI) and stochastic models (Black-Scholes, Heston), the system provides Fair Value (NPV), risk sensitivities (Greeks), and explicit 95% Confidence Intervals for simulation-based pricing. This allows Valuation Control Groups (VCG) and internal audit to directly observe and quantify valuation uncertainty.

02. VANILLA OPTIONS VALUATION

PRIMARY MODELS: BLACK-SCHOLES / LOCAL VOLATILITY
RISK IMPACT: DELTA/VEGA HEDGING ACCURACY & PNL ATTRIBUTION

The Vanilla Options module computes European option premiums and associated Greeks. A critical governance requirement is the prevention of arbitrage; hence, the engine utilizes SVI (Stochastic Volatility Inspired) surface parameterization. This ensures an arbitrage-free interpolation across strikes and maturities, strictly eliminating negative calendar spreads or butterfly arbitrages. Accurate volatility surface calibration directly impacts the robustness of the Delta and Vega hedging ratios, thereby minimizing unhedged exposures and unexpected PnL swings during market shocks.

03. EXOTICS & PATH-DEPENDENT PRICING

PRIMARY MODELS: MONTE CARLO SIMULATION (LONGSTAFF-SCHWARTZ)
RISK IMPACT: BARRIER TOUCH PROBABILITIES & SIMULATION NOISE RESERVES

Pricing path-dependent derivatives (e.g., Barrier and American Options) introduces significant non-linear risks. This module employs Monte Carlo simulations utilizing 10,000 paths and 252 time-steps as the regulatory baseline, alongside the Longstaff-Schwartz algorithm for early exercise valuation. The SSVI (Surface SVI) framework is heavily recommended to preserve no-arbitrage conditions across time, which is paramount for accurately estimating barrier-touch probabilities. Furthermore, the engine explicitly outputs the standard deviation and 95% Confidence Intervals of the Monte Carlo estimator. This transparency empowers risk controllers to assess simulation noise and mandate appropriate valuation reserves (Bid-Offer / Model Risk Reserves).

04. STRUCTURED PRODUCTS (ELNs)

PRIMARY MODELS: HESTON STOCHASTIC VOLATILITY / CALLABLE MC
RISK IMPACT: ISSUANCE FAIR VALUE & IMPLIED YIELD BENCHMARKING

The Equity-Linked Notes (ELNs) module provides pricing and yield-solving capabilities for complex Autocallable structures (e.g., Phoenix, Eagle). Given the long-dated maturity and complex payoff contingencies (Autocall, Coupon, and Capital Protection barriers), the engine utilizes the Heston Stochastic Volatility model to account for volatility skew and term structure dynamics. For Independent Price Verification (IPV), the engine features a "Solving Mode" that derives the implied coupon yield from the product's par value. This allows risk managers to benchmark internal issuance yields against secondary market observable levels, ensuring fair pricing and compliance with best execution policies.
